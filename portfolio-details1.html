Thermoacoustic Energy Harvester Research

What:

  • Designed, created physics models, and developed a standing wave Thermoacoustic Energy Harvester. This Harvests wated heat energy by converting the heat to accoustic energy that then generates Electrical Energy.

How:

  • Modeled Electrical and Mechanical aspets of a linear alternator as a Transfer Function to determine coupled Electromechanical System resonance for Maximum Power Transfer
  • Used Matlab to numerically solve impedance matching models, solving for max power transfer operational frequencies
  • Set up automated Data acquisition system with digital filters to collect and analyze, transducer data such as vibration frequencies and power output signals.

Results

  • The derived physicss model for the Thermoacoustic Engine matched experimental output and resonance frequencies within 10%
  • Engine was able to produce sufficient AC power to light an LED bulb with 25W waste heat resulting in a 30% efficient energy harvester
